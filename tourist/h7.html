About Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park, located in Assam, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its population of one-horned rhinoceroses. The park spans across lush grasslands and wetlands, and it is a major conservation success story for wildlife in India.

The park is also home to tigers, elephants, and a wide variety of birds and other wildlife, making it one of the most important wildlife sanctuaries in the world.
